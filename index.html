🎅 Christmas in July

At Jellystone this weekend: "Santa's Summer Vacation"

Your dates land on the final weekend of Jellystone's "Santa's Summer Vacation" theme (July 6–19) — expect Santa dropping by alongside Yogi, holiday-in-July crafts, and the everyday camp fun: the water zone (blasters, cannons, slides), pools & swimming hole, jumping pillow, mini golf, and shaving-cream whiffle ball. 🚗 Funspot is on the way home

Funspot (Weirs Beach) sits right on I‑93 — only ~15 min off the highway and barely a detour heading back toward Middleton, MA. Don't make it a special trip: aim to stop on the drive up or the drive home. Figure about 35–40 minutes of total detour time — worth it to visit the biggest arcade on the planet? Yes. It's also your ultimate rainy-day backup if the weather turns.

⭐ Closest to camp (5–15 min)

You could fill the whole weekend without driving more than 15 minutes.

Theme park

Story Land

🚗 3 min

Classic fairy-tale amusement park, best for ages ~2–10. Rides, a splash zone, and character meet-ups. Literally next door to camp.

Indoor · Rainy-day

Living Shores Aquarium

🚗 3 min

Hands-on interactive aquarium — touch tanks, stingrays, a bird aviary. Perfect hot-afternoon or rain backup, right beside Story Land.

🎢 Mountain coaster

Cranmore Mountain Adventure Park

🚗 15 min

North Conway's all-in-one: a mountain coaster, giant swing, soaring eagle zip, tubing, and an aerial adventure course. A top pick for the coaster you asked about.

💦 Waterfall · Wading

Diana's Baths

🚗 15 min

Short, flat, kid-friendly walk to a run of cascades and shallow pools. A White Mountains family classic.

🚂 Scenic train

Conway Scenic Railroad

🚗 12 min

Vintage train rides from North Conway. Short Valley trains for little ones; the Mountaineer through Crawford Notch for jaw-dropping scenery.

💦 Lake · Views

Echo Lake & Cathedral Ledge

🚗 15 min

Easy swimming beach below the cliffs, plus a short drive to the top of Cathedral Ledge for a huge valley view.

🥾 Scenic drive

Kancamagus Highway swimming

🚗 15 min to start

The famous "Kanc." Roadside stops: Lower Falls (swimming), Rocky Gorge, and the easy Sabbaday Falls walk.

🏔️ North of Glen — Jackson, Pinkham Notch & Mt. Washington (12–22 min)

Head up Route 16 toward the highest peak in the Northeast — with a pretty village and easy waterfalls along the way.

Summit adventure

Mt. Washington Auto Road

🚗 20 min

Drive yourself (or take a guided van tour) to the 6,288-ft summit. Bring a jacket — it's cold and windy up top even in July.

💦 Waterfall · Village

Jackson Falls & Village

🚗 12 min

Roadside cascades and pools right in the postcard village of Jackson — free, easy wading, and a covered-bridge photo op on the way up Route 16.

🥾 Easy trails · Bikes

Great Glen Trails

🚗 20 min

Flat, family walking and biking paths along the river at the base of the Auto Road. Bike and gear rentals on site.

💦 Waterfall

Glen Ellis Falls

🚗 20 min

Short walk to a dramatic 64-ft waterfall in Pinkham Notch. Quick, scenic, and easy.

✅ Quick trip tips

  • Don't sleep on the campground. Jellystone has a pool/splash area, mini golf, a jumping pillow, and Yogi character events on summer weekends — an easy low-cost day between outings.
  • Buy park tickets online in advance. Story Land and Santa's Village post daily calendars and summer weekend tickets are usually cheaper (and faster to enter) bought ahead.
  • Confirm hours before you drive. Attraction and lift hours shift week to week in summer — a 30-second check saves a wasted trip, especially for the 45–60 min spots.
  • Pack layers for Mt. Washington. The summit can be 30–40°F colder than the valley with fierce wind, even on a sunny July day.
  • Beat the crowds and heat. Arrive at theme parks and Diana's Baths near opening; save pools and indoor spots for the hot mid-afternoon.
  • Reserve dinner on weekends. Red Parka, Moat Mountain, and other favorites get slammed — call ahead or expect a wait.
